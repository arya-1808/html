Heritage Sites in India

Hawa Mahal,Rajasthan

The Hawa Mahal, or the "Palace of Winds," is an iconic five-storey pink and
red sandstone palace located in the heart of Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. Built in
1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh and designed by the architect Lal
Chand Ustad, this breathtaking structure is celebrated globally for its
unique honeycomb-like facade.

Torna Fort,Maharashtra

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandagad, is a large fort located in Pune district,
in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It is historically significant because it was the first fort
captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1646, at the age of 16.

Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh

Khajuraho is a historic town located in the Chhatarpur district of Madhya Pradesh, India, globally
renowned for its magnificent Khajuraho Group of Monuments. Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site,
these structures are acclaimed for their intricate Nagara-style architecture and stunning stone carvings
