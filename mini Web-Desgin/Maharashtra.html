Shaniwar Wada,Maharashtra

Shaniwar Wada is a historical fortification in the city of Pune,
India. Built in 1732, it was the seat of the Peshwas of the
Maratha Confederacy until 1818. The fort itself was largely destroyed
in 1828 by an unexplained fire, but the surviving structures are
now maintained as a tourist site.The Shaniwar Wada was normally the
seven-story capital building of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire

Gateway of India,Maharashtra

The Gateway of India is an arch-monument, completed in 1924, on the
waterfront of Mumbai, Maharashtra, India. It was erected to commemorate
the landing of King George V of the United Kingdom for his coronation as
the Emperor of India in 1911 at Strand Road near Wellington Fountain
It is used as symbolic ceremonial entrance to India for important
government personnel. .

Raigad,Maharashtra

Raigad Fort is a 17th-century mountain fortress in Maharashtra, India.
It served as the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati
Shivaji Maharaj, who was crowned here in 1674. Located 2,700 feet
high in the Sahyadri range, the fort features historic ruins like the
grand court, an ancient marketplace, and Shivaji Maharaj's tomb (Samadhi).
Visitors can reach the top via a steep 1,737-step trek or a 4-minute ropeway ride.
